This guide covers **Dabi's Strike battle style, Crazy Torch**. It does not cover original Technical Dabi.

Crazy Torch Dabi turns health into pressure. Gamma enhances Alpha and Beta, creates timed explosions around Dabi, and makes the whole kit threatening enough to overwhelm a target quickly. The tradeoff is severe: Gamma drains HP, Beta commits Dabi directly onto an enemy, and he has few defensive answers when that commitment goes wrong.

Season 17 gives him a stronger fallback plan than older guides suggest. Recent footage highlights improved Alpha damage and a much longer Gamma duration. However, a June 13 Season 17 test also shows Beta sometimes releasing grabbed enemies early. This guide therefore treats **Alpha and Gamma as the dependable core** and **Beta as high-value but currently inconsistent**.

## Season 17 quick verdict

- **Best identity:** high-pressure Strike brawler with strong close-to-midrange area damage.
- **Reliable Season 17 plan:** activate Gamma from a safe position, pressure with enhanced Alpha, and use Beta after an enemy commits or loses an escape.
- **Main risk:** Gamma spends HP while Beta can place Dabi inside the enemy team.
- **Current patch concern:** Beta may drop a target before completing its full sequence.
- **Beginner objective:** learn when to turn Gamma on and off before trying to force every Beta.

Crazy Torch is easiest when a teammate starts the fight for him. He is much less stable when he must cross an open sightline, activate Gamma, find a target, and survive the enemy team's response by himself.

## The basic game plan

### Before the fight

Stay one Beta movement away from cover. Identify:

- A corner, doorway, roof edge, or slope that can hide Gamma activation.
- An enemy who has already spent mobility.
- A teammate who can occupy the other two opponents.
- A retreat line that does not require running directly through the enemy team.

Do not activate Gamma simply because it is available. Its longer Season 17 duration is useful only if there is a fight to pressure. Activating too early converts your own HP into nothing.

### Opening the fight

Use enhanced Alpha to make opponents move. Its fire pillars cover useful horizontal space, and the hit area can catch targets above what the visual effect appears to show. Aim at:

- Healing or reviving players.
- Enemies landing from a movement skill.
- Doorways and narrow exits.
- The ground beside a moving target rather than only at their center.
- Opponents focused on your teammate.

Alpha is grounded. Jumping before the input prevents it from firing, so finish your landing before shooting.

### Committing with Beta

Beta is strongest as a punish, not as a blind opener.

Good Beta targets are:

- An enemy whose dodge or mobility skill was just spent.
- A player staggered by a Gamma explosion.
- Someone isolated from their teammates.
- A target climbing, healing, reviving, or locked in a long animation.
- An opponent already pressured by your teammate.

Bad Beta targets are:

- A full team standing together.
- An enemy next to armor, counter, or displacement tools.
- A target across open ground while your team is behind you.
- Anyone you can reach only by spending your final escape charge.

Because Beta can release a target early in Season 17, do not mentally spend damage that has not happened yet. Track the target throughout the grab and be ready to aim Alpha, dodge, or leave immediately if they fall out.

## What each skill is for

### Alpha — Flames of Resentment

Alpha creates fire pillars in front of Dabi and can be charged for wider coverage. The current data describes up to five pillars at maximum charge. More pillars make the attack easier to connect across an area; they do not mean that one target automatically takes five separate full hits.

Use unenhanced Alpha when:

- You need a quick shot and cannot safely activate Gamma.
- You want to check a corner or doorway.
- A target is already close enough that charging would lose the opening.

Use enhanced Alpha when:

- Gamma is active and the five-pillar version fires immediately.
- You want to pressure multiple paths at once.
- Beta is unsafe, unavailable, or behaving inconsistently.
- An opponent is moving vertically near a wall or ledge.

Season 17 footage emphasizes Alpha as a legitimate primary damage tool rather than filler between grabs. Its generous area makes it the safest answer when Beta cannot be trusted.

### Beta — Scorching Admonition

Beta launches Dabi upward, grabs a connected target, and continues the attack. Gamma greatly increases its approach range, making it both a burst tool and Dabi's main practical movement option.

Treat its two charges as separate jobs:

1. One charge can threaten, punish, or reposition.
2. The other should usually remain available to escape or chase after the fight changes.

In Season 17, teammates can still attack the grabbed target, but recent creator testing reports reduced teammate damage during the grab. The exact reduction is patch-sensitive. The practical lesson is simple: coordinate the grab as control and guaranteed contact, not as an invitation for everyone to dump their most valuable burst into a potentially reduced window.

The larger problem is the release bug. A target may fall out at the start or midway through the attack without an obvious interrupt. Until that behavior is fixed:

- Prefer Beta against enemies who have already spent their answer.
- Avoid using both charges back-to-back by habit.
- Do not rely on full Beta damage to finish a low target.
- Keep your camera on the opponent so an early release does not become a free punish.
- Use enhanced Alpha as the consistency fallback.

### Gamma — Crazy Torch

Gamma is the engine of the kit. It consumes HP over time, enhances Alpha and Beta, triggers smaller explosions during the effect, and produces a larger explosion when the effect ends.

Season 17 increased its useful duration. That gives Dabi a longer pressure window, but it also creates a common trap: leaving Gamma active through downtime and donating too much health.

Turn Gamma on when:

- A real fight is beginning within your effective range.
- You need the enhanced Beta distance to punish or escape.
- An enemy is committed near you and a timed explosion can control their next action.
- Your team is pushing and can prevent every opponent from focusing you.

Turn Gamma off early when:

- The enemy disengages behind hard cover.
- You need to heal or rotate.
- Your health is becoming the enemy's easiest win condition.
- You have already secured the down and no second target is close.
- Continuing the effect would force you to fight the next engagement while dangerously low.

Manually ending Gamma creates a smaller explosion. This gives the cancel defensive and combo value; it is not merely a way to stop losing health.

### Special Action — Unquenchable Flame

Dabi can revive himself while downed inside the safe area and releases fire during the action. It is powerful but not guaranteed.

Before using it, check:

- Are you inside the safe area?
- Is an enemy waiting close enough to interrupt or immediately down you again?
- Can a teammate create space first?
- Would crawling behind cover make the revive substantially safer?

Do not let the self-revive justify a reckless Gamma trade. Smart teams know the Special Action exists and will watch the downed Dabi.

## Recommended Season 17 level order

For a consistent ranked route:

1. **Alpha to level 4** for the first size and ammunition breakpoint.
2. **Beta to level 4** for faster reload.
3. **Gamma to level 4** for duration, reload, and area improvements.
4. **Alpha to level 9** for the strongest reliable neutral tool.
5. **Beta to level 9** for maximum punish and movement uptime.
6. **Gamma to level 9** last.

This route weights the Season 17 Alpha improvement and current Beta inconsistency more heavily than launch-era advice.

For an aggressive player whose Beta is connecting reliably:

1. Beta to level 4.
2. Alpha to level 4.
3. Gamma to level 4.
4. Beta to level 9.
5. Alpha to level 9.
6. Gamma to level 9.

A June 13 Season 17 gameplay source used the aggressive **Beta → Alpha → Gamma** priority. An older launch guide preferred **Gamma → Beta → Alpha**. The current recommendation splits the difference: take all three level-4 breakpoints, then choose Alpha for consistency or Beta for maximum commitment.

## Combo notation

- **Pulse** means one of Gamma's smaller timed explosions.
- **Gamma cancel** means manually ending Gamma to trigger the cancel explosion.
- **Melee 1** means the first normal melee hit, not the full string.
- **Enhanced** means Gamma is active.

Exact damage and timing vary with level, T.U.N.I.N.G., role bonuses, guard state, terrain, latency, prior knockdown buildup, and balance changes.

## Reliable practical routes

### 1. Enhanced Alpha pressure

**Gamma activation → enhanced Alpha → reposition → enhanced Alpha**

This is the Season 17 baseline. It does not require Beta to behave correctly, and it lets Dabi pressure while keeping both movement charges.

Move between shots. Standing still because the Alpha covers a large area makes Dabi an easy ranged target.

### 2. Pulse confirm

**Gamma pulse connects → Beta**

The pulse creates a short opening for the grab. This is more dependable than throwing Beta raw at a target who is watching you.

The Beta release bug can still end the sequence early, so be ready to stop after the pulse rather than chasing deep.

### 3. Safe pulse conversion

**Gamma pulse connects → enhanced Alpha**

Use this when Beta would carry you into the enemy team or when the grab has been inconsistent in the current match.

Aim the pillars across the target's likely dodge path.

### 4. Timed grab burst

**Begin Beta shortly before a Gamma pulse → pulse hits during the grab**

This is the signature Crazy Torch timing. The goal is to make the automatic explosion overlap the controlled target.

It requires awareness of the Gamma rhythm and is less reliable online than the simple pulse confirm. Practice the timing, but do not stare at the Gamma meter so long that you lose awareness of the other two enemies.

### 5. Close punish

**Melee 1 → Gamma pulse or Gamma cancel → Beta**

An older guide demonstrates a melee confirm into Gamma damage and then Beta. Treat it as conditional in Season 17:

- The opponent must be in a valid hit state.
- Beta must connect cleanly.
- No second enemy can interrupt the commitment.
- Dabi must have enough health to keep Gamma active.

If those conditions are not present, stop after the explosion and create space.

### 6. Gamma cancel escape

**Gamma cancel explosion → dodge or Beta toward cover**

This is a defensive sequence, not a damage combo. Cancel to interrupt close pressure, stop the HP drain, and leave while the opponent deals with the explosion.

Aim Beta at terrain or open space only when the route is clear. Auto-tracking toward a nearby enemy can turn an intended escape into another commitment.

### 7. End-of-Gamma trap

**Pressure near the end of Gamma → force the enemy to choose between retreating and contesting the large explosion**

Do not assume the final explosion will land. Strong opponents will disengage, use invulnerability, armor through the situation, or knock Dabi away.

Use the threat to claim space, finish a revive, deny a doorway, or separate a target from their team.

## Gamma health management

Crazy Torch players lose many fights before the opponent lands the decisive hit.

Use this health rule:

- **High health and guard:** activate Gamma to establish pressure.
- **Broken guard but healthy HP:** continue only if you have cover, a confirmed target, or a teammate controlling attention.
- **Low HP with guard remaining:** consider canceling anyway; guard does not refund the health Gamma is spending.
- **Low HP and no guard:** cancel, disengage, and heal unless one immediate action ends the fight.

Healing while Gamma continues to drain is inefficient. If the fight has paused, cancel first unless you are deliberately preserving an imminent explosion for a nearby threat.

Green cards and healing-oriented T.U.N.I.N.G. can offset some risk, but they do not replace good cancel timing.

## Movement and positioning

### Fight around short lanes

Crazy Torch is strongest where Alpha can cover exits and enhanced Beta can cross the remaining distance. Doorways, alleys, slopes, and roof edges are better than flat open ground.

### Do not climb into Alpha

Alpha cannot be used in the air. If you climb or jump toward a target, plan the landing before expecting to fire. Beta is the airborne option, but using it only to compensate for a bad jump can waste your escape.

### Use Beta movement selectively

Enhanced Beta travels far, but its tracking can pull Dabi toward an opponent. Before using it as movement, check the center of your screen and nearby targets.

### Approach from the fight's side

Running directly at the enemy team makes every projectile and displacement tool relevant. Enter from an angle after your teammate occupies their cameras. Dabi's strongest burst is close-range; his safest approach is not.

## Team-fight priorities

Prioritize:

1. An enemy reviving or healing in a confined area.
2. A mobile character who has spent their escape.
3. A ranged attacker focused on your teammate.
4. A low-guard target who can be forced through a Gamma explosion.
5. An isolated opponent Beta can grab without carrying you into two teammates.

Avoid:

- Grabbing a durable frontliner while their damage dealers aim at you.
- Activating Gamma before your team is ready.
- Chasing a released Beta target through open space.
- Letting the large final explosion tempt you to remain in a losing fight.

Communicate Beta targets. Even with reduced teammate damage during the grab, the control can let teammates reposition, reload, prepare the next hit, or remove pressure elsewhere.

## Matchup considerations

### Against mobile characters

Pressure with Alpha until they spend movement. Beta after the escape, not before it. If they remain airborne, use Alpha's generous vertical hit area near their landing rather than chasing with repeated jumps.

### Against armor and counters

Do not lead with Beta. Use Alpha and Gamma explosions to force the defensive tool, then commit during its recovery. A failed grab leaves Dabi close enough to receive the opponent's best answer.

### Against ranged teams

Move cover to cover before activating Gamma. Enhanced Alpha gives Dabi meaningful pressure, but it does not make him win an exposed firing line. Beta across open ground only when the target is distracted or their team cannot punish the landing.

### Against close-range brawlers

Use the Gamma pulse and cancel explosion as spacing tools. Do not accept a pure damage race while Gamma is already draining your health.

### In small final circles

Gamma becomes easier to convert because enemies have less room to leave its explosions. It also becomes more dangerous because every team can see Dabi. Let another player reveal themselves first, then enter from the side and preserve one Beta charge for the final reposition.

## Common mistakes

- Activating Gamma while rotating with no enemy in range.
- Treating Beta's full sequence as guaranteed in Season 17.
- Spending both Betas on one target and having no exit.
- Charging Alpha when a quick shot would secure the opening.
- Jumping before Alpha and losing the input because the skill is ground-only.
- Staying in Gamma until the large explosion regardless of health or positioning.
- Healing while unnecessary Gamma drain continues.
- Diving the nearest target instead of the isolated target.
- Depending on self-revive instead of crawling to cover first.

## Ten-minute Training routine

1. **Two minutes:** fire uncharged and charged Alpha at different elevations; learn how high the hit area reaches.
2. **Two minutes:** activate Gamma and move between Alpha shots without standing still.
3. **Two minutes:** watch the Gamma rhythm and convert a pulse into Beta, then into Alpha.
4. **Two minutes:** manually cancel Gamma and immediately escape with dodge or Beta.
5. **Two minutes:** start at partial health and practice ending Gamma before it leaves you inside realistic enemy finishing range.

Then play one match with a restriction: never spend the second Beta unless the first created a clear advantage. This teaches the resource discipline Crazy Torch needs more effectively than practicing maximum-damage strings against a stationary target.

## Final checklist

Before activating Gamma:

- Is a fight actually starting?
- Do I have cover and a retreat line?
- Which enemy has already spent mobility?
- Can my team occupy the other opponents?

Before pressing Beta:

- Is this a punish or a blind approach?
- Where will the animation leave me?
- What will I do if the target drops early?
- Am I preserving one charge to leave?

Crazy Torch Dabi is not only a grab character. In Season 17, his strongest practical play is to control the fight with enhanced Alpha and Gamma pressure, then use Beta when the opponent has already lost the right to escape.
